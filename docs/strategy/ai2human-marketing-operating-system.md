# AI2Human Marketing Operating System

## Core principle

AI2Human does not win by sounding like a polished enterprise deck.
AI2Human wins when the market can see the network moving in public.

The goal is not to explain everything.
The goal is to make three things obvious:

1. AI workflows still break on human steps.
2. AI2Human already has a product loop for those steps.
3. Real users, real submissions, real review, and real settlement are happening now.

## The category

Use this framing consistently:

- `AI2Human is the execution network for AI workflows.`
- `Agents can start the job. AI2Human helps finish the steps only humans can do.`
- `task -> human execution -> proof -> verify -> settle`

Avoid dropping back into:

- generic "future of work"
- generic freelancer marketplace language
- abstract "AI platform" wording
- over-claiming enterprise maturity

## What marketing needs to do right now

At this stage, marketing has three jobs:

1. Build category understanding
2. Show proof that the network is active
3. Drive participation into live tasks and live campaigns

This means marketing should focus less on polished brand theater and more on visible movement.

## The three content lanes

### 1. Narrative

Explain why AI needs a human execution layer.

Best topics:

- where agents fail in real workflows
- why proof and verification matter
- why settlement must be conditional
- why "fully autonomous" breaks at trust gates
- why human fallback is infrastructure, not patchwork

### 2. Product CTA

Tell people what to do now.

Best CTAs:

- bind X
- add contact email
- complete profile
- join a live contest
- submit an article
- claim a task
- message us if you build agents and want integration

### 3. Receipts

Show visible proof that AI2Human is alive.

Best receipts:

- new campaign launched
- participant count
- submitter count
- winners selected
- payout complete
- tx links
- admin screenshots
- report screenshots
- review summary screenshots

## Priority audiences

Do not spread attention across every possible audience at once.

Current priority order:

1. crypto-native builders and agent builders
2. active X users / operators who can participate in campaigns

Later audiences:

- enterprise workflow buyers
- governance / staking users as a primary acquisition audience

## Tone guidance

The product is serious, but the public voice should not feel dead.

Use:

- direct language
- concrete examples
- momentum
- screenshots
- urgency when a campaign is live

Do not default to:

- sterile whitepaper prose
- heavy enterprise jargon
- fake grandiosity
- long abstract intros

## The operating cadence

For the current stage, the baseline weekly mix is:

- 2 narrative posts
- 2 product CTA posts
- 2 receipts posts
- 1 founder-style thought or ecosystem interaction

This matters more than building a huge content calendar no one follows.

## The screen test

Before publishing a post, ask:

1. Does this make the category clearer?
2. Does this show the network moving?
3. Does this give someone a reason to act now?

If the answer is no to all three, do not post it.

## What to optimize for

The best marketing outputs right now are not "beautiful brand documents."

The best outputs are:

- screenshots worth reposting
- metrics worth quoting
- product loops worth explaining
- tasks worth joining
- integrations worth imagining

AI2Human should look alive, specific, and useful.
That is the real operating system.
